Building defects and causes
  • Water

    Water can cause damage and defects to the fabric of buildings either by undesired entry or the inability of water vapour to exit a building in a controlled way. This can result in primary degradation of building materials causing loss of intended functionality which may in turn lead to secondary degradation by fungal and mould growth. The cause of such defects can include the loss of the integrity of weatherproof finishes and damp proof membranes, leaking plumbing or drainage pipe work and the poor design and/or installation of vapour barriers. The correct identification of the causes of an issue, together with the design and implementation of appropriate remedial works are important for maintaining the value of the property and contents, together with the health and well-being of its occupants.

  • Structure

    The unintended movement of building structures causes cracking of building’s finishes and possibly the over stressing of structural elements. In extreme cases this can lead to the partial or total collapse of a building. Such movement can be caused by earthquakes, changes to the ground conditions under a building or the inadequacy of component structural elements the building. Many buildings display cracks of some sort and recognising the different types and causes is crucial to understanding the structural integrity and interventions that may be required.

  • Fire

    The performance of a building on fire is first and foremost critical to the life safety of its occupants and emergency responders, be it residential apartments or large commercial or industrial buildings. The passive fire resistance of a building is determined by its compartmental design, incorporated materials and finishes together with the fire load of its contents. The fundamental purpose of passive fire resistance together with appropriate active systems, such as sprinklers, fire shutter, smoke extract systems and the like is to enable the occupants of the building to escape to a place of safety. I have significant experience in passive fire design, inspection compliance with state regulations and insurance standards, together with the integration of active fire safety components and systems. I do not inspect the functionality or efficacy of active fire safety components or systems.

  • Acoustics

    Acoustic issues with buildings generally fall into three categories: firstly noise ingress into a building from and outside source, secondly, the transmission of noise between two or more parts of a build contained within the same overall envelope (neighbour noise) and thirdly issues with the acoustic environment within a particular space in a building, such as long reverberation time or echo. The measurement and analysis of many noise penetration issues is particularly important as the design of possible solutions is very dependent on the frequency spectrum or of the Intrusive sound. Having undertaken the acoustic design of new multifunctional performance spaces and remedial design solutions to reverberation and insulation problem, I am aware of the practical limitation in this field.
